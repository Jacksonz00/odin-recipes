Kalbi

Galbi (also known as Kalbi) is a staple in korean cuisine when it comes to their famous Korean BBQ. Kalbi is a marinated sweet and savory short ribs dish cooked right in front of you. During the time it takes to cook, the exchange of words between the guests as they take in a puff of the delighted aromas that emit from this cut of beef; there really is no better description as to how holy this experience may be for people.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 lb korean beef short ribs
  • 1 asain pear, peeled, cored, minced
  • medium sweet onion (yellow onion is fine as well); chopped and minced
  • 1 piece fresh ginger, peeled and minced
  • 7 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 green onion, chopped for garnish

Steps

  1. Rinse the short ribs under cold water. Let this soak in fresh cold water for 20-30 minutes to get rid of any impurities and bone fragments. (repeat if necessary)
  2. Meanwhile, make the marinade: In a blender, combine Asian pear, sweet onion, ginger, garlic, soy sauce, water, brown sugar, honey, sesame oil and blend until smooth and liquid
  3. Remove short-ribs from the water and pat dry
  4. Transfer to a clean container, or marinating bag, and pour the marinade over the short ribs. Mix well to insure even coating. Cover and marinate in the fridge for at least 6 hours, but preferably overnight
  5. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in either a Korean barbecue griddle or large cast iron skillet over medium heat.
  6. In batches, avoid overcrowding the cook surface, cook the short ribs to desired doneness; but at least 2-3 minutes. Flip only once to retain moisture.
  7. When the kalbi is almost done, cut the short ribs between the bones so you get individual "square" pieces
  8. Plate the kalbi and top with green onion garnish
  9. Enjoy with friends!
